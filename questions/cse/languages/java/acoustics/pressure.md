answer:
  - "330 × √2 ms⁻¹"
explanation: "The velocity of sound in an ideal gas depends on the absolute temperature ($T$) according to the relation $v \propto \sqrt{T}$. When the temperature is doubled, the velocity increases by a factor of $\sqrt{2}$. Furthermore, the velocity of sound is independent of pressure changes as long as the temperature remains constant. Therefore, halving the pressure has no effect, and the final velocity is $330 \times \sqrt{2} \text{ ms}^{-1}$."
